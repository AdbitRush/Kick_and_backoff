# Terms of Service Analysis

**Source: Kickbacks.ai/terms (v1.1, June 10, 2026)**

---

## The Critical Clause — Section 6.2

> *"the impression is generated by a **bona fide, human-initiated AI coding request** (not automated, scripted, or artificially generated)"*

### What qualifies

| Scenario | Qualifies? | Reason |
|----------|:----------:|--------|
| You type a prompt, Claude thinks | Yes | Human initiated, real task |
| You run coffee-brake.py during your session | Yes | You started it, real queries |
| A cron job fires queries while you sleep | No | Automated, not human-initiated |
| 10 parallel workers with no human at keyboard | No | Scripted, artificially generated |

### The safe interpretation

**You** press Enter (or start coffee-brake and leave). The *initiation* is human. The model waiting while you grab coffee is legitimate — Kickbacks counts the spinner time, not whether you're staring at it.

What is NOT legitimate: completely unattended loops, scheduled jobs, or server-side automation with no human session behind it.

---

## Section 3.4 — One Account Per Person

One Kickbacks account per person. Running the proxy on multiple machines is fine as long as it's the same account. Multiple people sharing one account = violation.

---

## Section 5 — Revenue Share

50% of ad revenue, paid monthly via Stripe. Minimum payout threshold may apply — check your dashboard.

---

## Practical Rules

1. **Route real work through the proxy** — don't generate synthetic queries just for impressions
2. **coffee-brake.py is borderline** — use it to extend a session you're already in, not as a standalone revenue machine
3. **Don't share your Publisher ID** — it's tied to your account; fraud on it is your liability
4. **Free models only change the cost, not the legitimacy** — routing through Nemotron instead of Claude Sonnet is fine

---

## Bottom Line

The arbitrage is in **cost reduction** (free models vs $15/M tokens), not in impression farming. Every query you'd have run anyway now costs $0. That's the sustainable play.
